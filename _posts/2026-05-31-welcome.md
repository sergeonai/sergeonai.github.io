This is the first post on *The Compounding Mind* — so let me explain what this place is for.

I write about things that compound: money, knowledge, habits, ideas. The name is deliberate. Compounding isn't just a financial concept — it's a way of thinking about time and accumulated advantage.

## What you'll find here

Most posts will fall into one of three areas: **Personal Finance** — frameworks for thinking about money, investing, and building wealth slowly; **Investing** — deep-dives into businesses, valuation, and portfolio thinking; **Productivity** — how I use AI tools in my day-to-day work, and what's actually worth automating.

## Why write at all?

Writing forces clarity. If I can't explain something clearly in a post, I probably don't understand it well enough.

> The best investment you can make is in your own knowledge. The returns compound just like the financial kind.

Thanks for reading. More soon.
