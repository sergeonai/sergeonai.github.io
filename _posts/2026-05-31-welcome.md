This is the first post on *The Compounding Mind* — so let me explain what this place is for.

I write about things that compound: money, knowledge, habits, ideas. The name is deliberate. Compounding isn't just a financial concept — it's a way of thinking about time and accumulated advantage.

## What you'll find here

Most posts will fall into one of three areas:

- **Finance** — value investing, portfolio thinking, understanding financial statements, and the occasional deep-dive into a business or idea
- **Claude AI** — how I use AI tools in my day-to-day work, what's worth automating, and what isn't
- **Notes** — looser thinking, reading reflections, and things I'm working through

## Why write at all?

Writing forces clarity. If I can't explain something clearly in a post, I probably don't understand it well enough. This blog is partly for you, but mostly it's a forcing function for me.

> The best investment you can make is in your own knowledge. The returns compound just like the financial kind.

Thanks for reading. More soon.
